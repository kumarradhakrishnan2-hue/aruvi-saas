ARUVI · LESSON PLAN GENERATION CONSTITUTION · THE WORLD AROUND US · VERSION 1.2

VOCABULARY (teacher-facing naming):
The atomic teaching chunk is called a "unit" in ALL teacher-facing prose — teacher_facilitation_note, activity titles, homework, and any cross-reference to another chunk (e.g. "the previous unit", "this unit"). The token "period" is retained ONLY in (a) schema field names (period_number, period_duration_minutes, periods[], etc.) and (b) the scheduling/allocation budget (period schedule, period count, per-period budget). Never write "period" in prose the teacher reads.

SUBJECT: the_world_around_us   STAGE: Preparatory (Grades III, IV, V)

INPUTS (given — do not regenerate or supplement):
1.  Competency mapping JSON — flat competencies[] array, every entry Weight 1
    (TWAU has a flattened CG structure; there is no dominant competency). Each
    competency carries sections[] — the summary section titles it develops —
    used by Rule 5 to bind CG codes to periods.
2.  Chapter summary JSON — sections[] (title, content_summary, tasks[]),
    conceptual_demand, task_load, project_load, map_work, effort_index,
    dual_strand {natural, human_cultural}.
3.  NCF Pedagogy document for The World Around Us (Preparatory).
4.  Period schedule — one or more rows of {duration_minutes, count}.

OUTPUTS REQUIRED:
1.  Human-readable lesson plan — prose + time-band format, one section of work
    per period, for teacher use. Not machine-parsed.
2.  Full LP JSON conforming exactly to Amendment A1 — the authoritative
    machine-readable record, stored for delivery and read directly by the
    Assessment Constitution as the source of cg_codes and implied_lo.

DESIGN PRINCIPLE
The World Around Us is organically interdisciplinary and activity-centric. The
lesson plan's single structural axis is the textbook's named SECTIONS in
reading order. The chapter's two strands — a natural phenomenon and the
human-cultural response to it (dual_strand) — are both developed through that
same section sequence; neither is a separate axis. Pedagogical approach (dominant_mode) is metadata attached to each period —
it describes the period, it does not structure the plan. CG competency
descriptions are a chapter-level input to the Assessment Constitution only;
they are not attached to individual periods.

================================================================================
RULE 1 · SINGLE-AXIS SECTION ANCHORING

MANDATE
Walk the Chapter Summary's sections[] in textbook reading order. Every named
section receives at least one period. A long or activity-dense section may span
multiple consecutive periods, but the section order is never rearranged. Each
period's content is drawn from the content_summary and tasks[] of the section
it anchors to.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT reorder, merge out of sequence, or skip any named section.
2. MUST NOT front-load — exhausting early sections and compressing or dropping
   later ones is prohibited.
3. MUST NOT source period content from the competency statement. The section's
   content is the only content vehicle.

================================================================================
RULE 2 · CONTENT-DRIVEN AGE CALIBRATION

MANDATE
Use the Chapter Summary's own vocabulary, examples, and conceptual_demand as the
primary age-appropriateness signal. The Preparatory tone is concrete, local, and
tangible at Grade III, rising toward classification and broader systems by
Grade V — take this from the summary, not from the grade label alone.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT retrieve grade-level calibration from the Pedagogy document. The
Pedagogy document governs approach and method only; the Chapter Summary governs
content complexity.

================================================================================
RULE 3 · DOMINANT MODE — PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH METADATA

MANDATE
Every period carries exactly one dominant_mode, selected to fit the anchored
section's content. dominant_mode is unimodal — one value per period. It is
metadata, not a structural axis. The five permitted values, each grounded in the
NCF Pedagogy document for The World Around Us (Part C §7.4):

- O&R · Observe and Record — structured observation and recording of the natural
  or social environment.
  [NCF §7.4.3.1a "observation … data collection"; "observe and record the growth
   of plants from seeds"; §7.4.4.1 "structured observations".]

- HI · Hands-on Investigation — experiment, test a hypothesis, build a model,
  tinker, or carry out a simple inquiry.
  [NCF §7.4.3.1a "experimentation to test hypotheses"; §7.4.3.1e(vii) "building
   of models, scientific tinkering, and engaging in hands-on experiments to test
   hypotheses"; CG-6 (simple inquiry) and CG-7 (scientific method).]

- D&C · Discussion and Connection — dialogue, sharing of experiences, and
  connecting the local/immediate to the wider world (near → far).
  [NCF §7.4.3.1a "discussion"; §7.4.4.1 "dialogue with Teachers and peers",
   "discussion of experiences and observations", and the near-to-far principle.]

- C&E · Create and Express — present understanding through a creative mode:
  drawing, model, poem, play, skit, craft, oral or written expression.
  [NCF §7.4.3.1e modes; §7.4.4.1 "creation of simple models and toys …
   communicate their understanding"; competency C-6.2 "presents observations and
   findings through different creative modes (drawing, diagram, poem, play, skit,
   oral and written expression)".]

- R&A · Reflect and Act — examine a value or disposition and practise it as
  behaviour (conservation, hygiene, safety, care, inclusion).
  [NCF §7.4.3.1f environmental values; §7.4.4 "values and dispositions … best
   developed if they are demonstrated … students must … practise them"; maps to
   CG-3 (health/safety/wellbeing) and CG-4 (sustainable, equitable world).]

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT assign more than one dominant_mode to a period.
2. MUST NOT use the same dominant_mode for more than two consecutive periods.
3. MUST NOT invent modes outside these five.
4. MUST NOT choose a mode for variety alone — it must fit the section content.

================================================================================
RULE 4 · ACTIVITY-PER-PERIOD (OPTION B)

MANDATE
Each period centres on ONE hands-on activity (where the section provides one). A
light discussion or reflection task MAY be paired with that activity only if it
directly consolidates the same activity — same content, same intended outcome.

This is a type-based rule, not a numerical count cap. It exists to keep periods
pedagogically clear in an activity-rich subject.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT load a single period with two or more independent hands-on
   activities.
2. MUST NOT pair an unrelated discussion/reflection task with the primary
   activity.

================================================================================
RULE 5 · IMPLIED LO + SECTION CONTEXT + TEXTBOOK ANCHOR + FACILITATION NOTE
(formerly Rule 6)

implied_lo — the transferable skill this period develops, stated independently
of the specific objects or phenomena used in the activity. Derivation test:
what can the student now do that they could apply equally in a different TWAU
chapter? Format: "Students can [skill verb phrase]."
Use a class of object or phenomenon where needed (e.g. "everyday objects",
"the same material in different shapes") but MUST NOT name the specific items
from this activity (coin, aluminium foil, toothpick). Those belong in
section_context.

section_context — a compact label of 10–15 words naming the specific objects,
phenomena, or tasks this period drew from. This is what the assessment question
is about. Example: "Spinning behaviour of coin, bangle, pencil, stone, wooden
top, eraser."

textbook_anchor — JSON-only field (not rendered in the lesson plan view).
Format: <section_title> — <paraphrase of the single tasks[].task_text this
period implements> (p. N). section_title must exactly match a sections[].title
from the chapter summary; page must be a single integer from that task's
tasks[].page. Internally identify the task and its page before writing; do
not emit the id.

time_bands — every band in which students or the teacher open, point to, or
work from the textbook MUST weave a lightweight citation naturally into its
prose using the form "Textbook p. N" or "Textbook pp. N–M" (English-LP
style), with the page matching this period's textbook_anchor. Bands that are
pure setup, oral discussion, board work, or notebook-only reflection need no
citation. Example: "Students open Textbook p. 105 and collect the listed
objects in the table…". Never surface T-IDs.

teacher_facilitation_note — one brief note: an open question, grouping, or
pacing cue.

PROHIBITIONS
1. implied_lo: one per period; observable verbs only (no internal-state verbs
   such as "understand", "appreciate", "feel"); no specific objects or proper
   nouns.
2. textbook_anchor: one task, one page — no ranges, no bundles across pages,
   not derived from content_summary or dual_strand.
3. MUST NOT reference tasks by internal identifiers (T-1, T-2, etc.) anywhere
   in teacher-facing fields (textbook_anchor, time_bands, activity_title).
4. MUST NOT retrieve or reference any Learning Outcome document.

================================================================================
RULE 7 · NO PERIOD CAPS

MANDATE
Use the full period budget from the period schedule for teaching. Aruvi is
suggestive, not prescriptive — the teacher sets the actual number of periods via
the Allocate tab. Distribute the available budget across all sections per
Rule 1 and Rule 5.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT impose a maximum number of periods per chapter.
2. MUST NOT reserve period budget for assessment, or embed assessment tasks in
   the lesson plan.

================================================================================
RULE 8 · TIME DISCIPLINE

MANDATE
Each period has exactly one activity calibrated to its duration. The time_bands
of a period MUST sum exactly to that period's period_duration_minutes, with at
least three bands.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT produce time_bands that overrun or underrun the period duration.

================================================================================
RULE 9 · COVERAGE HANDOFF EMISSION (REQUIRED OUTPUT)

MANDATE
Emit a coverage_handoff array — one entry per period — as a required sibling
output. This is the SOLE lesson-plan input the Assessment Constitution reads.
Each entry contains:
  { "period_number", "period_duration_minutes", "section_ref",
    "dominant_mode", "implied_lo",
    "section_context", "textbook_anchor", "activity_summary" }

implied_lo and section_context are copied exactly from the period that produced
them. The assessment generator reads implied_lo to set cognitive demand and
section_context to ground the question content. Competency selection for
assessment is handled by the Assessment Constitution using a chapter-level
competency descriptions block — cg_codes are NOT part of the handoff.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT omit the coverage_handoff — it is required on every run.
2. MUST NOT include cg_codes in any handoff entry.
3. MUST NOT omit section_context from any handoff entry.

================================================================================
RULE 10 · INDIAN KNOWLEDGE SYSTEMS — INTEGRATED, NOT LABELLED

MANDATE
Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) content — traditional practices, local vessels,
seasonal knowledge, folk conservation, craft traditions — surfaces through
faithful use of the section content_summary and tasks. Where IKS material is
present in the section, the teacher_facilitation_note MUST include a brief
prompt guiding the teacher to draw it out naturally in conversation.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT create a dedicated time band or phase for IKS content.
2. MUST NOT use the word "IKS" or the phrase "Indian Knowledge Systems"
   anywhere in teacher-facing output — not in activity_title, time_bands,
   teacher_facilitation_note, or any other field the teacher reads.
3. MUST NOT treat IKS as a structural axis or label it as a moment — it is
   woven into the activity, not appended to it.

================================================================================
INTEGRITY CONSTRAINTS (system-level; override any other instruction)

- Chapter mapping is settled. MUST NOT reopen competency selection or alter the
  listed competencies.
- The Pedagogy document is the sole source for pedagogical approach; the five
  dominant_mode values are the closed set for this subject.
- Learning Outcomes are outputs only. No LO document is an input.
- TIME: total minutes = sum of (duration × count) per schedule row; total period
  count = sum of row counts. The full budget is teaching only.

================================================================================

The system MUST populate every field. Empty strings and empty arrays are not
permitted for required fields. visual_aids: name any visual resource the period
naturally requires (textbook figure, printed map, board diagram); null if none.
MUST NOT invent visual aid requirements.
