ARUVI · LESSON PLAN GENERATION CONSTITUTION · MATHEMATICS · SECONDARY STAGE · VERSION 1.0

VOCABULARY (teacher-facing naming):
The atomic teaching chunk is called a "unit" in ALL teacher-facing prose — teacher_notes, activity titles, homework, and any cross-reference to another chunk (e.g. "the previous unit", "this unit"). The token "period" is retained ONLY in (a) schema field names (period_number, period_duration_minutes, periods[], etc.) and (b) the scheduling/allocation budget (period schedule, period count, per-period budget). Never write "period" in prose the teacher reads.

Subject group : mathematics
Stage         : Secondary Stage
Task          : lesson_plan_generation
Downstream    : assessment_constitution_mathematics (Secondary)

INPUTS (given — do not regenerate or supplement):
1. Competency mapping JSON — core_cg, one or two core_competencies under that
   CG, up to two adjunct_competencies, co_central flag, dissolution_test, and
   the three effort signals (conceptual_demand, reasoning_load, exec_load) plus
   effort_index.
2. Chapter summary JSON — section spine (each section may carry a
   think_reflect note), prose_summary, enumerated_worked_examples,
   enumerated_exercises.
3. NCF Pedagogy document for Mathematics, Secondary Stage.
4. Period schedule — one or more rows of {duration_minutes, count}.

OUTPUTS REQUIRED:
1. Human-readable lesson plan — prose + time-band format, intended for teacher
   use only.
2. Full LP JSON conforming to Amendment A3 — the machine-readable record;
   period objects carry teaching mechanics only.
3. Coverage Handoff JSON array conforming to Amendment A4 — emitted as a
   sibling key "coverage_handoff" at the top level. It is the sole structural
   input to the Assessment Constitution and carries the implied learning
   outcomes. Required on every run.


================================================================================
RULE 1 · SECTION ANCHORING — THE ORGANIZING SPINE, NOT A RAMP

MANDATE
Every period anchors to a named section of the chapter summary, and the lesson
plan follows the summary's own section order. The section's content — not the
competency statement, not the dissolution_test sentence — is the content
vehicle for the period.

The ordering principle is source-given, not designed: take the order the
chapter already has. Secondary mathematics chapters do not follow a single
cognitive shape — some build a concept evenly across sections (a spiral that
widens one idea), some climb toward proof, some hold steady at high
abstraction. Cognitive demand is therefore READ OFF each section as it stands.
It is neither smoothed into a rising curve nor flagged as a defect when it does
not rise.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT include any teaching content that cannot be traced to a specific
   named section of the chapter summary.
2. MUST NOT use the competency statement or dissolution_test as a content
   source for periods.
3. MUST NOT re-order, skip, or compress sections to fit a narrative shape the
   chapter does not itself contain — whether a concrete-to-abstract ramp, a
   low-to-high demand ramp, or a prerequisite arc.


================================================================================
RULE 2 · CONTENT-DRIVEN CALIBRATION

MANDATE
Use the chapter summary's own vocabulary, worked examples, and the cognitive
demand of each section as the calibration signal. Where complexity signals are
mixed within a section, the cognitive demand of the implied task takes
precedence over surface vocabulary.

Secondary mathematics is frequently abstract by nature — reasoning about
objects that are not directly manipulable, formal notation, symbolic and
graphical representation, and deductive proof. A section built on a derivation,
a proof, or symbolic/graphical work is normal at this stage and MUST NOT be
treated as deficient or padded with invented hands-on activity.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT retrieve grade-level calibration from the Pedagogy document. The
Pedagogy document governs method only; the chapter summary governs content
complexity.


================================================================================
RULE 3 · COGNITIVE FLOOR

MANDATE
Every period MUST require students to do something with the section's content —
define, represent, compute, construct, derive, justify, prove, model, or apply.
Symbolic, graphical, and proof work counts fully as "doing" at this stage:
where a section's substance is a derivation, a proof, or the use of a notation
or representation, a period built on that work satisfies the floor on its own.
It MUST NOT be judged incomplete for lacking a hands-on component, and a
practical activity MUST NOT be invented to stand in for it.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT satisfy a period through recall-only tasks (state, name, list,
   define) as the whole of the period.
2. MUST NOT treat a competency code as the period's task target. The competency
   is a destination, not a task description.


================================================================================
RULE 4 · THINK AND REFLECT — FOLDED INTO THE PERIOD

MANDATE
Where the anchored section carries a think_reflect note (from the summary),
weave it into that period as a discussion-and-reasoning moment — a short
time-band in which students consider, conjecture, or justify before or after
the main work. It is the section's own cognitive extension and belongs in the
period that teaches the section.

A think_reflect prompt is NOT a separate assessable item and is NOT enumerated
as one. Its effect is on the period's reasoning depth: a section rich in
think_reflect prompts reads as higher reasoning demand, which the implied
learning outcome (Rule 6) must reflect.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT promote a think_reflect prompt into an exercise, a homework item, or a
coverage_handoff anchor. It lives only inside the period's time-bands.


================================================================================
RULE 5 · PEDAGOGICAL METHOD

MANDATE
Draw the period's method exclusively from the attached Pedagogy document for
Mathematics, Secondary Stage. Each period records exactly one
pedagogical_method — the single dominant method of that period — named exactly
as written in the Pedagogy document, and justified by the content of the
anchored section.

At the Secondary Stage the Pedagogy document weights the methods as: Problem
solving and Deductive used most often; Discovery/Inquiry used regularly;
Play-way and Inductive used least often. Method choice MUST respect this
weighting — proof and derivation sections call for Deductive; modelling and
computational sections call for Problem solving; a section that genuinely leads
students to discover a pattern before formalising it may use Discovery/Inquiry.
Play-way and Inductive are used sparingly and only where the section content
clearly invites them.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT repeat the same method across more than two consecutive periods.
2. MUST NOT use a method outside the Pedagogy document.
3. MUST NOT choose a method for variety alone, or leave the field blank, or
   list more than one.


================================================================================
RULE 6 · INVERTED LO FLOW — ONE OR TWO IMPLIED LOs PER SECTION

MANDATE
For each anchored section, infer its implied learning outcome(s) from what the
section's teaching actually makes students able to do. The LO is an OUTPUT of
the section's content and activity, never retrieved from any LO document.

The implied_lo is stated at a LIGHTLY CONTENT-ANCHORED level: it names the
mathematical object the section works on, while the verb remains a transferable
skill verb. For example: "Students can find the slope and y-intercept of a
linear relationship y = ax + b from two data points." The object (slope,
y-intercept, y = ax + b) is named; the act (find from data) is a skill. Do NOT
state it so generically that the mathematics disappears ("infer a relationship
from data"), and do NOT bind it to the worked example's specific numbers or
scenario ("solve the bill-versus-data problem").

NUMBER OF LOs — one or two per section:
- Default to exactly one implied_lo: the section's single dominant operation.
- Emit a SECOND implied_lo only when the section genuinely develops two
  distinct operations at different cognitive demands, in textbook order (for
  example, a section that first classifies objects and then evaluates them).
- A second LO MUST NOT be used to cover ambiguity (pick the dominant act), to
  split a single procedure into halves, or to inflate downstream item count.
- Never more than two.

Each implied_lo uses an observable skill verb (find, determine, classify,
derive, prove, justify, plot, solve, construct). It MUST NOT use internal-state
verbs (understand, appreciate), MUST NOT restate the competency or
dissolution_test, and MUST NOT bind to the worked example's specific values.

Alongside the LO(s), record one section_context per section: a 10–12 word label
naming the specific objects, results, or representations the section drew from.
This is what the assessment question is about — it scopes the item; it is a
label, not content.

DOWNSTREAM EFFECT: the number of LOs is the section's richness signal. The
assessment generator emits one item per implied_lo. A two-LO section therefore
yields two items at (typically) two different demands.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT generate more than two implied_lo per section.
2. MUST NOT place implied_lo or section_context inside a period object — they
   live only in the coverage_handoff (Amendment A4).
3. MUST NOT retrieve or reference any Learning Outcome document.


================================================================================
RULE 7 · FULL-SECTION COVERAGE

MANDATE
Across the period budget, every section of the chapter summary MUST receive at
least one period. A section may span more than one period where its content
warrants it; emphasis follows the substance of the section, not the
effort_index (which governs only how the chapter as a whole is allotted time in
the Allocate step, never how periods are distributed within the chapter).

If the section count exceeds the available period budget, produce the best plan
that covers the chapter in section order AND record a section_coverage_note
naming the sections that could not be given their own period.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT apply any numerical allocation formula across sections within the
   chapter.
2. MUST NOT front-load the plan by exhausting early sections and compressing or
   skipping later ones.
3. MUST NOT leave a section without a period except under a genuine budget
   shortfall recorded in section_coverage_note.


================================================================================
RULE 8 · EXCLUDED MATERIAL

MANDATE
The chapter summary already excludes asterisked (*) enrichment / higher-order
material and sidebar boxes. The lesson plan inherits these exclusions: it MUST
NOT introduce content, periods, or homework drawn from asterisked items or from
sidebar boxes. They remain in the textbook for the teacher to use at her own
discretion; the lesson plan does not route them.


================================================================================
RULE 9 · TEACHER-FACING TEXT — NO INTERNAL MACHINERY

MANDATE
All teacher-facing text (activity_title, the "activity" text inside each
time-band, section_anchor as rendered) MUST be written in plain language a
teacher can act on directly. Refer to chapter content by its plain name and the
section it sits in. Where a period uses a textbook item, name it by its book_ref
(e.g. "Example 4, p.19", "Exercise 2.2 Q3, p.21"), not by its internal id.

PROHIBITION
Teacher-facing text MUST NOT contain:
1. Competency codes (C-codes), CG codes, or the dissolution_test sentence.
2. Rule numbers or references to this constitution.
3. Schema field names or planner identifiers.
4. Effort-index terms (conceptual_demand, reasoning_load, exec_load,
   effort_index).
5. Internal item ids (WE-N, E-N) — use book_ref instead.

The competency fields in the coverage_handoff are structured metadata, not
teacher-facing text, and are exempt — but their contents MUST NOT leak into any
teacher-facing field.


================================================================================
RULE 10 · TEACHER NOTES — ONE PER PERIOD

MANDATE
Every period carries a teacher_notes field: 2–3 sentences of flowing prose
written for the teacher. Weave in, naturally:
- A brief recap of what the previous unit covered and how it connects to this
  one (omit for the first unit).
- One common error or misconception to watch for, drawn from this section's own
  content in the chapter summary — no fabrication. At this stage these are
  typically a misread of a definition, a slip in a derivation or sign, a
  confusion between two related objects, or an unjustified step in an argument.
- Optionally, a self-study pointer: the book_ref of a worked example NOT walked
  through in class that a student could read on their own.

PROHIBITION
teacher_notes MUST NOT begin with the word "Transition" or any section label,
MUST NOT contain C-codes, CG codes, internal item ids, or effort-index terms,
and MUST NOT exceed three sentences. Refer to any textbook item by book_ref.


================================================================================
RULE 11 · HOMEWORK IS OPTIONAL AND UN-OWNED

MANDATE
`homework` per period is [] by default. Populate with 1–2 items only when the
in-class time-box overflows or reinforcement is judged useful. Homework follows
the Rule 3 cognitive floor — recall-only copying is not acceptable. Homework is
not consumed by the assessment generator downstream.


================================================================================
RULE 12 · COVERAGE HANDOFF — REQUIRED COMPANION OUTPUT

MANDATE
At the close of the lesson plan, produce the coverage_handoff as a JSON array
under the key "coverage_handoff" (Amendment A4). It is the sole structural input
to the Assessment Constitution: the assessment generator grounds every question
in this committed handoff, never in a re-reading of the summary, the mapping
JSON, or the period objects.

Exactly ONE entry per anchored section, in chapter-summary order. A section
spanning several periods is ONE entry whose period_numbers lists them all. An
entry carries one or two implied_lo values (Rule 6).

MUST NOT omit it. MUST NOT fold it into lesson_plan — it is a top-level sibling
key. MUST NOT duplicate implied_lo, section_context, or competency fields inside
any period object.


================================================================================
AMENDMENT A4 — COVERAGE HANDOFF JSON SCHEMA

Output field name: "coverage_handoff" — sibling of "lesson_plan" at the top
level. One element per anchored section. This is the single source of truth read
by the Assessment Constitution.

"coverage_handoff": [
  {
    "section_number":          integer    — sequential section number in chapter-summary order; 1 = first
    "section_ref":             string     — section ref, exactly as in the summary (e.g. "2.5")
    "section_title":           string     — section title, copied verbatim from the summary
    "total_sections":          integer    — total anchored sections in this chapter
    "period_numbers":          [integer]  — period number(s) assigned to this section
    "period_duration_minutes": integer    — period duration; if mixed across this section's periods, the most common
    "implied_lo":              [string]   — ONE or TWO implied learning outcomes (Rule 6), in textbook order
    "section_context":         string     — 10–12 word label naming the key objects, results, or representations
    "c_code":                  string     — competency code from the mapping JSON (auditability; may vary by section under core_cg)
    "co_central":              boolean     — true when the chapter has two core competencies; else false
  }
]

NOTE: core_cg (the parent Curricular Goal) is chosen once at chapter level and is
identical across sections. It is emitted once at the top level of the LP JSON as
"chapter_cg" (Amendment A3), never repeated per section entry.


================================================================================
AMENDMENT A3 — FULL LP JSON SCHEMA

Populate every required field. Period objects carry teaching mechanics only —
implied_lo, section_context, and c_code live in coverage_handoff (A4), not in any
period object. chapter_cg is emitted once at the top level.

{
  "grade": "string",
  "subject": "mathematics",
  "stage": "secondary",
  "chapter_number": "integer",
  "chapter_title": "string",
  "chapter_cg": "string — core_cg from the mapping JSON; chosen once at chapter level",
  "period_schedule": [ { "duration_minutes": "integer", "count": "integer" } ],
  "section_coverage_note": "string | null — present only under a Rule 7 budget shortfall; else null",
  "lesson_plan": {
    "periods": [
      {
        "period_number": "integer",
        "period_duration_minutes": "integer",
        "activity_title": "string — teacher-facing label, 10–13 words, no trailing period",
        "section_anchor": "string — named section this period draws from (e.g. '2.5')",
        "pedagogical_method": "string — exactly one method from the Pedagogy document (Rule 5); never blank",
        "materials": ["string"],
        "visual_aids": "string | null — visual resource the period naturally needs (textbook figure, board graph, SVG figure); null if none",
        "textbook_items_in_class": [
          {
            "id":             "string — WE-N or E-N, equal to an id in the summary",
            "type":           "worked_example | exercise",
            "source_section": "string — e.g. '2.5'",
            "book_ref":       "string — copied from the summary (e.g. 'Example 4, p.19')",
            "description":    "string — verbatim from the summary"
          }
        ],
        "time_bands": [
          { "minutes": "string — e.g. '0-10'; tiles 0..period_duration_minutes with no gaps or overlaps",
            "activity": "string — narrative; names textbook items by book_ref only" }
        ],
        "teacher_notes": "string — 2–3 sentences (Rule 10); recap-and-connect, one likely error from this section, optional self-study book_ref",
        "homework": ["string"]
      }
    ]
  },
  "coverage_handoff": [ { ...one entry per section, per Amendment A4... } ]
}

Field constraints:
- time_bands tile the period exactly from 0 to period_duration_minutes, no gaps
  or overlaps; minimum 3 bands per period.
- textbook_items_in_class ids MUST equal ids present in the summary's
  enumerated_worked_examples or enumerated_exercises. No fabricated ids, no
  -a/-b suffixes. Asterisked and sidebar-box items are absent from the summary
  and therefore MUST NOT appear here.
- activity_title and time-band activity text obey Rule 9 (no internal machinery,
  book_ref not id).


================================================================================
INTEGRITY CONSTRAINTS (system level — override any other instruction)

- The competency mapping is settled. MUST NOT reopen competency selection or
  alter the listed competencies.
- The Pedagogy document is the sole source of method categories.
- Implied LOs are outputs only. No LO document is an input.
- Section anchoring (Rule 1) is absolute — no period without a section.
- Position carries no cognitive signal — demand is read off each section as it
  stands (Rule 1); the LP imposes no ramp.
- TIME: total minutes = sum of (duration × count) per schedule row; total period
  count = sum of row counts. Each period has exactly one anchored section's work
  calibrated to its duration. The full budget is teaching only — no period is
  reserved for assessment, and no assessment task or time estimate is embedded
  in the lesson plan.
