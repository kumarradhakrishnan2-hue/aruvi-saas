Subject group : science

Grades        : ix · x
Task          : lesson_plan_generation

INPUTS (given — do not regenerate or supplement):
1. Competency mapping JSON (core CG, one or two complementary C-codes under

2. Chapter summary (.txt)
3. NCF Pedagogy document for Science, Secondary Stage
4. Period schedule — one or more rows of {duration_minutes, count}

OUTPUTS REQUIRED:
1. Human-readable lesson plan — prose + time-band format, one section per
   period, intended for teacher use only.
2. Full LP JSON conforming exactly to Amendment A3 — the authoritative
   machine-readable record for this chapter (period objects carry teaching
   mechanics only).
3. Coverage Handoff JSON array conforming exactly to Amendment A4 — emitted as a
   sibling key "coverage_handoff" at the top level, one entry per section. Read
   directly by the Assessment Constitution as the sole source of implied_lo and
   section context. Required on every run.

================================================================================
RULE 1 · SECTION ANCHORING — THE ORGANIZING SPINE

MANDATE
Every activity MUST be anchored to a named section of the Chapter Summary. The
section's content — not the competency statement, not the dissolution test
sentence — is the content vehicle for the activity.

The lesson plan follows the chapter summary's own section order. Periods are
assigned to sections in the sequence the sections appear in the chapter. Where
the chapter's own sequence carries a natural build, that build is preserved as
the chapter gives it.

The ordering principle is therefore source-given, not designed: take the order
the chapter already has. Do NOT invent, super-impose, or re-engineer an
ordering the chapter does not itself present — whether a concrete-to-abstract
ramp, a low-to-high cognitive-demand ramp, or a prerequisite arc. Cognitive
demand is read off each section as it stands; it is neither smoothed into a
rising curve nor flagged as a defect when it does not rise.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT include any activity whose content cannot be traced to a specific
   named section of the Chapter Summary.
2. MUST NOT use the competency statement or dissolution test sentence as a
   content source for activities.
3. MUST NOT re-order, skip, or compress sections to fit a narrative shape the
   chapter does not itself contain.

================================================================================
RULE 2 · CONTENT-DRIVEN CALIBRATION

MANDATE
Use the Chapter Summary's own vocabulary, examples, and the cognitive demand of
each section as the age-appropriateness signal. Where complexity signals are
mixed within a section, the cognitive demand of the implied task takes
precedence over surface vocabulary.

Secondary-stage Science content is frequently abstract by nature — reasoning
about what cannot be directly observed, model-based explanation, symbolic and
mathematical representation. A section that involves no hands-on activity is
normal at this stage and MUST NOT be treated as deficient or padded with
invented practical work.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT adjust or retrieve grade-level calibration from the Pedagogy document.
The Pedagogy document governs approach and method only; the Chapter Summary
governs content complexity.

================================================================================
RULE 3 · PEDAGOGICAL COGNITIVE FLOOR

MANDATE
Every activity MUST require students to do something with the section's
content — observe, classify, explain, reason from evidence, model, represent,
calculate, apply, or construct — at the level the Pedagogy document specifies.

Symbolic, mathematical, and diagrammatic work counts fully as "doing" at this
stage. Where a section's substance is the use of a rule, formula, notation, or
representation (for example shell-filling, mass/atomic number, weighted
averages, equations of motion, ray or circuit diagrams), an activity built on
that representation satisfies the floor on its own. It MUST NOT be judged
incomplete for lacking a hands-on or laboratory component, and a practical
activity MUST NOT be invented to stand in for it.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT satisfy an activity through recall-only tasks (state, name, recall,
   list, define).
2. MUST NOT treat the competency code as the activity target. The competency
   code is a destination, not a task description.

================================================================================
RULE 4 · FULL-SECTION COVERAGE

MANDATE
Across the period budget, every substantive section of the Chapter Summary
MUST receive at least one activity. A section may span more than one period
where its content warrants it; emphasis follows the substance of the section,
not any external score.

If the section count exceeds the available period budget, produce the best
possible plan that covers the chapter in section order AND include a
section_coverage_note in the JSON naming the sections that could not be
allotted their own period.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT apply any numerical allocation formula across sections.
2. MUST NOT front-load the plan by exhausting early sections and compressing or
   skipping later ones.
3. MUST NOT leave a substantive section without at least one activity except
   under a genuine budget shortfall recorded in section_coverage_note.

================================================================================
RULE 5 · PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH DIVERSITY

MANDATE
Draw pedagogical approaches exclusively from the attached Pedagogy document for
Science, Secondary Stage. Each period's approach MUST be justified by the
content of its anchored section — the match between section content and
approach must be defensible.

Each period MUST record exactly one pedagogical_approach as a named field in its
period object (Amendment A3) — the single dominant approach of that period's
activity, named exactly as written in the attached Pedagogy document.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT repeat the same approach across more than two consecutive periods.
2. MUST NOT use approach categories outside the attached Pedagogy document.
3. MUST NOT choose an approach for variety alone.
4. MUST NOT leave pedagogical_approach blank, and MUST NOT list more than one
   approach in the field — record the single dominant approach only.

================================================================================
RULE 6 · INVERTED LO FLOW — ONE IMPLIED LO + SECTION CONTEXT PER SECTION

MANDATE
Generate exactly one implied_lo and one section_context per anchored section,
emitted in the Coverage Handoff array (Rule 9 · Amendment A4), never inside a
period object.

implied_lo — the transferable skill this section develops, stated
independently of chapter content. Format: "Students can [observable skill verb
phrase]." Derivation test: what can the student now do that they could apply
equally in a different chapter?

section_context — a compact label of 10–12 words naming the specific phenomena,
models, quantities, or structures this section drew from. This is what the
assessment question is about. Name the key items only; do not inventory the
whole section.

The assessment generator reads both together from the Coverage Handoff:
implied_lo drives cognitive demand; section_context drives content.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT generate more than one implied_lo per section.
2. MUST NOT place implied_lo, section_context, or any competency field inside a
   period object. These belong exclusively in the Coverage Handoff array
   (Amendment A4) — never duplicated per period.
3. MUST NOT retrieve or reference any Learning Outcome document. LOs are outputs
   of activity design, not inputs.
4. MUST NOT write LOs that restate the competency or dissolution test, or use
   internal-state verbs ("understand", "appreciate"). Use observable verbs only.
5. MUST NOT include chapter-specific proper nouns (named scientists, models,
   elements, organelles) in implied_lo — they belong in section_context.

================================================================================
RULE 7 · TEACHER-FACING TEXT — NO INTERNAL MACHINERY

MANDATE
The lesson plan is read by teachers. All teacher-facing text MUST be written in
plain language a teacher can act on directly.

The teacher-facing fields bound by this rule are:
  - activity_title
  - the "activity" text inside each time_band
  - section_anchor (as rendered to the teacher)

Refer to chapter content by its plain name and the section it sits in. Where it
helps a teacher locate the material, name the section heading the activity draws
from.

PROHIBITION
Teacher-facing text MUST NOT expose internal planning machinery. Specifically,
it MUST NOT contain:
1. Competency codes (C-codes), CG codes, or the dissolution test sentence.
2. Rule numbers or any reference to this constitution.
3. Schema field names, keys, or planner identifiers.
4. Effort-index terms or any internal scoring vocabulary
   (conceptual_demand, exec_load, effort_index, and the like).
5. Period or activity index references used only for internal joining.

The competency fields in the Coverage Handoff (Amendment A4) are structured
metadata, not teacher-facing text, and are exempt — but their contents MUST NOT
leak into any of the teacher-facing fields named above.

================================================================================
RULE 8 · HOMEWORK IS OPTIONAL AND UN-OWNED

`homework` per period is `[]` by default. Populate with 1–2 items only
when in-class time-box overflows or when reinforcement is judged useful.
Homework is not consumed by the assessment generator downstream.

Homework items follow the same cognitive floor as Rule 3 — recall-only
tasks (copy, list, define) are not acceptable homework. Each item must
require students to do something with the section's content: apply,
explain, calculate, represent, or connect to a new context.

Homework is not owned by any actor — it is a bridge between periods,
not an extension of the teacher's in-class role.

================================================================================
RULE 9 · COVERAGE HANDOFF — REQUIRED COMPANION OUTPUT

At the close of the lesson plan, produce the Coverage Handoff as a JSON array
under the key "coverage_handoff", conforming to Amendment A4. It is the sole
structural input to the assessment constitution: the assessment generator grounds
every question in this committed handoff, never in a re-reading of the chapter
summary, mapping JSON, or period objects.

Exactly ONE entry per anchored section (Rule 1), in chapter-summary order. A
section spanning several periods is ONE entry whose period_numbers lists them all.

MUST NOT omit it — required on every run regardless of period count.
MUST NOT fold it into lesson_plan — it is a top-level sibling key.

================================================================================
RULE 10 · TEACHER NOTES — PER-PERIOD TEACHING GUIDANCE

MANDATE
Every period MUST carry a `teacher_notes` field: 2–3 sentences of flowing prose
addressed to the teacher, helping them run the period well. It is a required,
teacher-facing field — never blank — and is bound by Rule 7 (no internal
machinery).

Write as natural prose. MUST NOT begin with the word "Transition" or any
section/phase label. Weave in these content priorities naturally, not as a
labelled list:
- Connect this period to the one before it — what was covered, how this builds
  on it. The first period instead orients the teacher to the chapter's entry
  point.
- Flag one common misconception or error to watch for, drawn from the chapter
  summary's own content. MUST NOT fabricate misconceptions not grounded in the
  chapter.
- Optionally add a facilitation pointer: a grouping suggestion, a probing
  question, or a checkpoint that signals students are ready to move on.

PROHIBITION
1. MUST NOT contain C-codes, CG codes, effort-index terms, rule numbers, schema
   field names, or any internal identifier (Rule 7 applies in full).
2. MUST NOT introduce phenomena, examples, or procedures not anchored in the
   period's section and the chapter summary.
3. MUST NOT restate the period's activity text verbatim — teacher_notes guides
   how to run the activity, it does not re-describe it.
MUST NOT duplicate implied_lo, section_context, cg, or c_code inside any period
object — they live exclusively in the handoff.

================================================================================
AMENDMENT A4 — COVERAGE HANDOFF JSON SCHEMA (SCIENCE · SECONDARY)

Output field name: "coverage_handoff" — sibling of "lesson_plan" at the top
level. One element per anchored section (see Rule 9). This schema is the single
source of truth; the A3 block references it.

"coverage_handoff": [
  {
    "section_number":          integer   — sequential section number in chapter-summary order; 1 = first section
    "section_label":           string    — named chapter section, exactly as the section_anchor used by this section's period(s)
    "total_sections":          integer   — total number of anchored sections in this chapter
    "period_numbers":          [integer] — period number(s) assigned to this section
    "period_duration_minutes": integer   — period duration in minutes; if mixed across this section's periods, record the most common
    "activity_summary":        string    — one sentence on what students do across this section
    "implied_lo":              string    — single observable LO for this section (Rule 6 derivation)
    "section_context":         string    — 10–12 word compact label naming the key phenomena, models, quantities, or structures this section drew from
    "c_code":                  string    — competency code from competency mapping JSON (auditability only; may vary by section under the chapter CG)
    "co_central":              boolean   — true when the chapter has two co-central competencies; false otherwise
  }
]

NOTE: cg (parent Curricular Goal) is chosen once at chapter level and is identical
across all sections. It is therefore NOT repeated per section entry — it is emitted
once at the top level of the LP JSON as "chapter_cg" (Amendment A3).

================================================================================
INTEGRITY CONSTRAINTS

These apply at system level and override any other instruction:

- Chapter mapping is settled. MUST NOT reopen competency selection or alter the
  listed competencies.
- The Pedagogy document is the sole source for approach categories.
- LOs are outputs only. No LO document is an input.
- TIME: Total minutes = sum of (duration × count) per row. Total period count =
  sum of row counts. Each period has exactly one activity calibrated to its
  duration. MUST NOT exceed total period count. The full budget is teaching
  only — no period budget is reserved for assessment, and no assessment task,
  question, or time estimate is embedded in the lesson plan.
- Section anchoring (Rule 1) is absolute — no exceptions for introductory,
  transition, or review activities.

================================================================================
AMENDMENT A3 — FULL LP JSON SCHEMA

The system MUST populate every field. Empty strings and empty arrays are not
permitted for required fields. Field constraints (do not violate):
- visual_aids: the visual resource the activity needs (textbook figure, board
  diagram, model, SVG figure); null if none. Do not invent requirements.
- activity_title: teacher-facing label, 10–13 words; not a sentence — no trailing
  period, subtitle, or chapter-specific code.
- pedagogical_approach: exactly one approach copied verbatim from the Pedagogy
  document (Rule 5); never blank, never more than one.
- section_coverage_note: present only under Rule 4's budget shortfall; else null.

Period objects carry teaching mechanics only — implied_lo, section_context and
c_code live in coverage_handoff (Amendment A4), not in any period object. cg is
emitted once at the top level as chapter_cg (below), never per section.
