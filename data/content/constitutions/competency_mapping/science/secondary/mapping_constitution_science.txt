Competency mapping
================================================================================

Rule 1 | competency list — development method
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The competency assignment for each Secondary Stage Science chapter (Grade IX / X) is
determined by a two-step process applied to every chapter. 

Step 1 — Chapter summary reading & CG identification

Pass 1: Before consulting the CG document or any framework document, the system MUST read
the chapter summary section by section.

Pass 2: Open the CG document. The single CG whose domain description matches the chapter's
subject matter is selected. Selection is made from CG-level headings only; C-code
definitions are not opened at this step. CG-5 through CG-8 are eligible only if no CG-1
through CG-4 domain matches — which applies only to orientation, cross-linkage,
India-contribution, or frontier/epistemology chapters. The selected CG is locked.

Step 2 — C-code selection: only the C-codes under the locked CG are read. A maximum of two
C-codes are selected — those whose definitions the chapter summary substantively addresses.
A second C-code requires independent justification from a distinct strand of the chapter,
not as a vehicle for the first. The dissolution test sentence is then written: "This chapter
builds the student's ability to [verb] [object] by [cognitive mechanism]." co_central is set
to true when, and only when, two C-codes are selected.


Rule 2 | Secondary-Stage application guidance
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Secondary CG set (cg_secondary_science.txt) is partitioned by domain — CG-1 matter at
the atomic level, CG-2 the physical world, CG-3 the living world at the cellular level,
CG-4 interconnectedness of organisms and environment. Most secondary chapters therefore
have one obvious home CG. The following rules govern the cases where this is not
immediately clean.

2a | Whole-chapter domain match (not section-summing)
Pass 2 selects the CG that matches the chapter's subject matter as a whole — NOT the union
of the domains its individual sections touch. A chapter may contain a section that, read in
isolation, looks like a different CG (e.g. a cell chapter whose opening section explains the
microscope, resolution, and convex lenses — superficially CG-2 optics, or CG-8
instrumentation). Such sections are instrumentation or scaffolding in service of the
chapter's actual subject; they do NOT redirect the CG lock. Judge the chapter by what it is
about, not by what tools or asides it passes through.

2b | Same-CG co-central constraint
A second (co-central) C-code MUST come from the same locked CG. A chapter may carry a
genuinely distinct second strand that belongs to a NEIGHBOURING CG — for example, a CG-3
cell chapter that also teaches the levels of organisation (cell → tissue → organ → organism),
which lives under CG-4. Cross-CG co-centrality is NOT representable in this schema: record
such a strand as tertiary in the justification prose if useful, but do not place it in
primary and do not switch the lock. The strand is not lost — it survives in the chapter
summary and is available to downstream lesson-plan and assessment generation.

2c | Single-competency, cognitively dense chapters
A secondary chapter may legitimately map to ONE C-code while being cognitively dense — the
abstraction characteristic of this stage (reasoning about the unobservable, mathematisation,
model-building) raises cognitive demand without broadening competency coverage. Do NOT force
a second C-code to "reflect" a hard chapter. Breadth is a competency question; difficulty is
captured separately by the effort-index signals (conceptual_demand, activity_count,
demo_count, exec_load). The Journey Inside the Atom chapter is the canonical example: a
single competency (C-1.1), high cognitive demand.

2d | Introductory / deferred strands
Secondary chapters frequently seed a strand that the textbook explicitly defers to higher
grades ("you will learn this in detail later"). Such a strand still qualifies as co-central
when it is (i) carried in its own named section(s), and (ii) cognitively distinct from the
primary strand — even if treated at introductory depth. It does NOT qualify when it appears
only as a passing mention inside a section governed by the primary C-code. Apply judgement:
a sectioned, distinct strand counts; a one-line aside does not.

COMPETENCY MAPPING JSON SCHEMA — SCIENCE (SECONDARY)
================================================================================

The "stage" field MUST be "secondary" for all Grade IX and Grade X chapters.

Example A — single-competency chapter (co_central = false)

{
    "stage": "secondary",
    "subject": "science",
    "grade": "ix",
    "chapter_number": 8,
    "chapter_title": "Journey Inside the Atom",
    "min_viable_periods": 0,
    "orientation_chapter": false,
    "co_central": false,
    "primary": [
      {
        "cg": "CG-1",
        "c_code": "C-1.1",
        "justification": "The chapter's whole domain is atomic structure via the Bohr model and the combining capacity (valency) that follows from electronic configuration — the 'atomic structure (Bohr's model) and properties (valency)' clause of C-1.1. The periodic-table / compound-formation clause of C-1.1 is not developed here; it is the domain of the sibling chapter (Atomic Foundation of Matter). The chapter's four sub-topics (model evolution, atomic/mass number and configuration, valency, isotopes/isobars) are all facets of atomic structure and serve this single competency; no distinct second strand exists within CG-1 (mixtures C-1.2 and chemical equations C-1.3 are absent)."
      }
    ],
    "dissolution_test": "This chapter builds the student's ability to determine the subatomic composition, electronic configuration, and valency of an atom by reconstructing how successive experimental evidence (cathode rays, gold-foil scattering, the neutron) reshaped atomic models and by applying shell-filling (2n²) and octet rules."
}
