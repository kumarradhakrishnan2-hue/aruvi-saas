Chapter 1 — The Ever-Evolving World of Science serves as the opening orientation chapter of the Grade 7 Curiosity science textbook. It frames science not as a collection of facts to be memorised, but as a living process of questioning, experimenting, and discovering. The chapter positions Grade 7 as a continuation of the inquiry-based journey begun in Grade 6, now asking deeper questions about how things work, why events happen the way they do, and what patterns exist in nature. It emphasises that science extends beyond the classroom and connects to environmental responsibility and the sustainability of the planet.

The introductory section provides a thematic overview of the entire textbook by weaving together the topics students will encounter across subsequent chapters. It previews the study of acidic, basic, and neutral substances through everyday examples such as sour fruits and turmeric stains. It then connects this to the exploration of electrical circuits and the classification of materials as metals and non-metals. The chapter introduces the idea of reversible and irreversible changes, using examples like a battery running out, ice melting, and fruits ripening, and connects this theme to the flow of heat in phenomena ranging from a melting ice cube to a melting glacier and the water cycle. The overview also touches on the biological changes of adolescence and the life processes essential to both animals and plants, highlighting that all living things must obtain nutrients and exchange gases to survive. The chapter draws attention to the measurement of time and motion and to the nature of light, shadows, and reflection, culminating in a preview of Earth, Moon, and Sun movements and the phenomena of day, night, and eclipses. Throughout, the chapter reinforces the interconnectedness of all these science topics, showing how ideas in one domain illuminate questions in another.

1.1 Happy Exploring! is a brief motivational section that transitions from the book overview to active participation. It conveys the idea that great scientists are distinguished not just by the answers they find but by the quality of questions they ask, echoing the Grade 6 theme of being a "whys person." The section contains a student activity.

Activity 1.1: Question the Answer inverts the typical school exercise by presenting students with four unusual answers and asking them to generate the creative questions that could have produced those answers. The activity is designed to encourage divergent, open-ended thinking as a foundational scientific habit of mind.
