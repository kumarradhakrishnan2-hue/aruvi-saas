Reflection in plane mirrors
Checks whether the class can transfer what this period built. Each item names the outcome it tests.
LEARNING OUTCOME
States the law of reflection and applies it to find an unknown angle.
MCQ · UNDERSTANDING visual: figure ▸
A ray of light hits a mirror at 30° to the surface. What is the angle of reflection, measured from the normal?
A60°✓ correct — measured from the normal, not the surface
B30°reveals: student measured from the surface (common error)
C90°reveals: confuses the normal with the mirror plane
D45°reveals: guessed the midpoint without applying the law
LOOK FOR
Whether the student measures the angle from the normal. If they pick B, reteach where the angle is measured from before moving on.
LEARNING OUTCOME
Relates reflection to a familiar everyday observation.
SHORT RESPONSE · APPLICATION
In your own words, state the law of reflection and give one everyday example where you see it.
EXPECTED ELEMENTS
Angle of incidence = angle of reflection · both measured from the normal · a valid example (mirror, still water, polished surface).
LEARNING OUTCOME
States the law of reflection and applies it to find an unknown angle.
EXPLAIN · APPLICATION
Why does a periscope let you see over a wall? Draw a ray diagram and explain using the law of reflection.
SCAFFOLD
Prompt: where does the light enter, and how many times does it reflect before reaching the eye?

Screen 5 · Start declaration (with inline version choice)
What an unstarted slot shows, reached straight from the dashboard's "pick a chapter to begin" row. "Which chapter are you starting?" lists the saved plans. Where a chapter has more than one version, the choice is offered here, in the flow — reading and choosing happen in the same place, motivated by the same intent (she's about to teach). Each version offers a full read-only preview (the chapter reader, Screen 4, including its assessment suite) so she can read before committing, then come back here to choose. The tap binds the chapter to this section, starts tracking, and locks that version for the chapter.
