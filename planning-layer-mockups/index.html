Aruvi · Planning layer
Two tabs · finalized model · mockups against planner §K / §L · 2026-06-27
Static visual mockups in the app's warm-paper design language (real globals.css tokens). The product has two tabs: My Plans (daily execution + the readiness setup that gates everything) and Generate (allocate → generate, in one tab). Screens below run in the order a teacher meets them. Not wired — reference for build.
Tab 1 of 2
My Plans
The operational home and the default first screen every day. Cross-scope (spans every subject & section). Owns readiness (weekly schedule + annual budget) and daily execution (the lesson pointer). No subject·grade label, and deliberately no budget number here — nothing that reads like tracking the teacher.
Gate: Generate stays locked until both the weekly schedule AND the annual budget are filled in here.

The first piece of real setup. Four fields only — day, grade, section, period duration. No dates, no holidays, no period-of-day numbers. The durations entered here are reused everywhere (they seed the period buckets in Generate).

Tab 2 of 2 · was called "Allocate" — now folded in
Generate
The whole act of getting a chapter ready, in one tab: choose chapters → set their time (budget ledger) → Generate Lesson Plan. Single-scope — it always operates on one subject·grade (the scope chip, top-right). Allocation is the on-ramp; the payoff is the plan, so the tab is named for the plan.
One pathway, no bypass: readiness → choose → allocate → Generate Lesson Plan. Opening the tab is passive (shows the ledger); every forward step is a deliberate tap.

Reflection in plane mirrors
Checks whether the class can transfer what this unit built. Each item names the outcome it tests. No marks, no scoring — a teaching aid.
LEARNING OUTCOME
States the law of reflection and applies it to find an unknown angle.
MCQ · UNDERSTANDING visual: figure ▸
A ray of light hits a mirror at 30° to the surface. What is the angle of reflection, measured from the normal?
A60°✓ correct — measured from the normal, not the surface
B30°reveals: student measured from the surface (common error)
C90°reveals: confuses the normal with the mirror plane
D45°reveals: guessed the midpoint without applying the law
LOOK FOR
Whether the student measures the angle from the normal. If they pick B, reteach where the angle is measured from before moving on.
LEARNING OUTCOME
Relates reflection to a familiar everyday observation.
SHORT RESPONSE · APPLICATION
In your own words, state the law of reflection and give one everyday example where you see it.
EXPECTED ELEMENTS
Angle of incidence = angle of reflection · both measured from the normal · a valid example (mirror, still water, polished surface).
MATHEMATICS · MIDDLE / PREP — these stages have no learning outcome in the data (structural link only). The LO line is then absent, not blank — no empty "LEARNING OUTCOME" label, no placeholder. Everything else is identical. (Maths secondary does carry an LO, so it shows the line like the others.)
