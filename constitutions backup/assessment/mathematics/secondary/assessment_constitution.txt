ARUVI · CHAPTER ASSESSMENT GENERATION CONSTITUTION · MATHEMATICS · SECONDARY STAGE · VERSION 1.0

Subject group : mathematics
Stage         : Secondary Stage   ·   Grades : ix · x
Task          : assessment_generation
Upstream      : lesson_plan_constitution_mathematics (Secondary) — coverage_handoff (Amendment A4)
Inputs        : coverage_handoff + Chapter Summary (exactly two; both required)
Textbook      : Ganita Manjari (Grade IX) — LTHC design, transition-to-proof

================================================================================
RULE 1 · GOVERNING PURPOSE

MANDATE
Generate tasks that test whether the student can perform the mathematical
operation each section develops. Every question MUST trace to an implied_lo of
the section it tests, and require an observable demonstration — a computed
result with working, a constructed argument, a classification with
justification, a plotted or read graph, or a derived relationship.

PROHIBITION
1. No questions asking whether a student understood, appreciated, or felt
   something — demonstration, not self-report.
2. MUST NOT test content recall independently of the section's implied_lo.
3. MUST NOT derive any architectural decision (length, format, ordering,
   demand) from a section's POSITION in the chapter. Secondary mathematics
   chapters do not ramp uniformly — a chapter may be a flat spiral, a
   proof-marathon, or abstract-but-low-justification, and all three MUST
   produce defensible assessments through the same demand-driven rules.

================================================================================
RULE 2 · INPUTS AND OUTPUTS

INPUTS (two, both required)
1. coverage_handoff — from the Secondary LP (Amendment A4). One entry per
   anchored section: section_number, section_ref, section_title,
   total_sections, period_numbers, period_duration_minutes, implied_lo (ONE or
   TWO values), section_context, c_code, co_central. The parent Curricular Goal
   arrives once at chapter level (chapter_cg in the LP JSON), NOT per section.
   Read as committed — do NOT re-derive, re-order, or supplement.
   · Emit chapter_cg once at chapter level, never per question. c_code is kept
     per question (the LP permits a complementary C-code, so it may vary by
     section).
2. Chapter Summary (JSON) — the section spine, prose_summary,
   enumerated_worked_examples, and enumerated_exercises. The prose_summary is
   the source of truth for question content: it supplies the actual scenarios,
   numbers, expressions, definitions, and structures that ground each question
   (Rule 3). Worked-example METHODS may be used to shape a question; their
   specific numbers and scenarios MUST NOT be copied (Rule 3).

OUTPUTS (three, all mandatory)
1. Section-structured assessment, items in coverage_handoff order.
2. Diagnostically intentional MCQ distractors (Rule 7).
3. A guide block per question (Rule 9), structured JSON, visible on demand.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT reference the Curricular Goals doc, Chapter Mapping JSON, Pedagogy
doc, any Learning Outcome document, or any source outside the two inputs; MUST
NOT re-evaluate competencies, re-open mapping, or alter the committed
coverage_handoff. The effort_index (CD×2 + reasoning×2 + exec×1.5) is a
within-grade time-allocation signal for the LP only — it is NOT an assessment
signal and MUST NOT inform format, count, or demand here.

================================================================================
RULE 3 · CONTENT GROUNDING — TAG SHAPES · CONTEXT SCOPES · SUMMARY SUPPLIES

MANDATE — three roles, never conflated:
  · cognitive_demand (Rule 4) SHAPES the item — question type and depth (Rule 5).
  · section_context SCOPES the item — names and fences it to one slice of the
    chapter. It is a label, not content.
  · the section's prose_summary slice SUPPLIES the item — the real scenarios,
    numbers, expressions, definitions, and structures for the stem,
    visual_stimulus, correct answer, and distractors.
A question built from section_context alone, without the summary's concrete
material, is non-compliant. A question that COPIES a worked example's specific
numbers, named entities, or wording — rather than generating a fresh instance
using the same method — is also non-compliant.

ADJACENT PULL (bounded)
Each item is OWNED BY and TESTS exactly one section. The correct answer and the
tested operation MUST come from that section's own slice. Earlier-section
material may be used as supporting scaffold (later sections build on earlier
ones in the same chapter) — as a distractor exploiting a genuine cross-section
confusion, or a scenario needing an earlier method — never as the thing
assessed. "Which section does this test?" MUST have one answer.

================================================================================
RULE 4 · COGNITIVE DEMAND TAG — THE GOVERNING HINGE

MANDATE
For each implied_lo, classify it on the Recall–Understanding–Application–
Analysis–Evaluation spectrum and record the result as cognitive_demand on the
question for that LO. This tag is the single governor of format (Rule 5). It
MUST be derived from and consistent with the implied_lo and the section's
summary slice — it makes the LP's demand explicit and auditable, never
reopening or recalibrating it. Where a section carries two implied_lo at
different demands, each gets its own tag and its own item.

Reading guide (operation, not position):
  identify · name · state · recognise · recall              → Recall
  describe · explain (one-step) · represent · classify      → Understanding
  calculate · evaluate (a polynomial) · solve · plot ·
    determine · find · model · use…to find · construct      → Application
  compare · analyse · infer · distinguish · derive ·
    generalise a pattern · explain why                      → Analysis
  prove · justify · judge · critique · decide between ·
    evaluate (an argument/method)                           → Evaluation
Where vocabulary and implied task conflict, the implied task's demand governs.
Note "evaluate" is read two ways: evaluating a polynomial at a value is
Application; evaluating an argument or method is Evaluation. The implied_lo's
object disambiguates.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT assign the tag from position or from neighbouring sections; MUST NOT
leave it blank or use a value outside the five-point spectrum.

================================================================================
RULE 5 · DEMAND-TO-FORMAT MATCHING

MANDATE — format follows the tag, never position:
  Recall / Understanding     → MCQ        (determinate answer; errors
                                            diagnosable via distractors, Rule 7)
  Application                → NUM or SCR  (NUM where the answer is a computed
                                            value reached by shown working —
                                            the default for evaluate / solve /
                                            find; SCR where the demonstration is
                                            a short worded procedure, modelling
                                            step, or representation without a
                                            single numeric answer)
  Analysis / Evaluation      → ECR        (no single answer; quality lives in
                                            the written reasoning, derivation,
                                            or proof)
  Integrative / cross-       → OPEN_TASK  (several operations on a novel
  operational (co_central)                 scenario; vehicle from the
                                            Mathematics menu, Rule 8)

co_central = true: a section integrating both central operations is the
OPEN_TASK and MUST require reasoning across both at once (Rule 8).

NUM is the math-native Application format: a numerical/algebraic answer the
student reaches by visible multi-step working, where selection from options
would reveal the steps that are the object of the task. Use it for evaluate,
solve, find a and b, model-and-compute, and plot-and-read items. Reserve SCR
for Application demonstrations that are procedural or representational rather
than single-answer numeric.

PER-LO COUNT (the LO count is the richness signal)
Emit exactly ONE item per implied_lo in the coverage_handoff. A one-LO section
yields one item; a two-LO section yields two items, typically at two different
demands and therefore two different formats (e.g. an MCQ classifying
polynomials by degree + a NUM evaluating one at a value). The LP has already
made the richness judgment by emitting one or two LOs — this constitution does
NOT re-open it, does NOT add bonus / mixed-review / chapter-wrap items, and does
NOT split one LO into two items.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT assign format from position, from the implied_lo's verb as a mere
label, or for variety; MUST NOT emit more or fewer items than there are
implied_lo values; MUST NOT add an anchor MCQ to an extended item by default.

================================================================================
RULE 6 · REASONING FLOOR

MANDATE
The assessment as a whole MUST contain at least one extended-reasoning item
(one ECR or one OPEN_TASK), guarding against a recognition-only assessment on a
procedural or computational chapter. If no implied_lo reaches Analysis,
Evaluation, or the integrative band, lift the single highest-demand LO to ECR
(or OPEN_TASK only where the chapter genuinely integrates operations), and note
the lift in that guide block. The lift changes the format of an EXISTING item;
it does NOT add an item, preserving one-item-per-LO (Rule 5).

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT emit an all-MCQ/NUM/SCR assessment; MUST NOT lift more than one LO;
MUST NOT manufacture integration a chapter lacks — ECR is the default lift.

================================================================================
RULE 7 · MCQ DISTRACTOR DESIGN

MANDATE
Every MCQ has exactly three diagnostic distractors (four options). Each is one
nameable mathematical error: a misconception about the section's object (e.g.
counting the constant term as a coefficient); a confusion between two chapter
concepts (e.g. linear vs quadratic, slope vs intercept, growth vs decay); a
predictable slip (sign error, off-by-one in a pattern's nth term, transposing a
and b); or recall of a related-but-wrong definition from the section's slice.

PROHIBITION
No true/false; no implausible, arbitrary, or out-of-chapter distractors; no
"none of the above" / "all of the above".

================================================================================
RULE 8 · OPEN TASK MENU — MATHEMATICS

MANDATE
Where Rule 5 or 6 calls for an OPEN_TASK, select its format ONLY from this menu,
justify the choice in the guide block, and state that the teacher may substitute
any other menu format. Each format is self-contained and classroom-answerable
(Rule 10):
  · Model-and-solve (build a linear expression/equation from a scenario, then
    compute and interpret)
  · Pattern generalisation (extend a sequence, derive its nth-term rule, justify)
  · Data-to-relationship (find a relationship y = ax + b from given data points,
    then interpret the constants)
  · Graph construction and comparison (plot lines, read off slope/intercept,
    compare families) — set graph_paper = true (VS-6); the green grid is
    rendered behind the figure, and a "plot this yourself" variant uses a blank
    green plane (visual_stimulus = null)
  · Multi-representation translation (move a relationship between table, symbol,
    graph, and word form and reconcile them)
  · Proof or derivation (establish or justify a result using chapter
    definitions and methods — the transition-to-proof vehicle at this stage)
  · Procedure / argument evaluation (judge a worked method or claim, identify
    the flaw, repair it)
co_central = true: the single OPEN_TASK is the integrative demonstration of both
operations and MUST NOT be split.

PROHIBITION
No format outside the menu; no selection for variety (it must be the most
natural vehicle for the implied_lo); never more than one OPEN_TASK per
assessment.

================================================================================
RULE 9 · GUIDE LAYER

MANDATE
Produce a guide block per question, as structured JSON (not prose), visible on
demand. By type:
  MCQ        — guide.MCQ.what_each_option_reveals (one entry per non-correct
               option, naming its mathematical error) · learning_outcome{section}
               · inclusivity.
  NUM        — expected_answer (top-level: the verified numeric/algebraic
               result) · method_one_line (top-level: the one-sentence chapter
               method) · guide.NUM: learning_outcome{section} · inclusivity.
  SCR        — expected_elements (top-level: components a complete response
               needs) · guide.SCR: learning_outcome{section} · inclusivity.
  ECR        — look_for (top-level: markers of a strong response/derivation/
               proof) · guide.ECR: learning_outcome{section} · inclusivity.
  OPEN_TASK  — guide.OPEN_TASK: format_type · format_rationale ·
               what_this_demonstrates · reading_the_scaffold ·
               strong_vs_weak_markers · inclusivity · learning_outcome{section}.

inclusivity is REQUIRED in every guide block, all five types: 1–2 sentences on
the alternative pathways by which different students demonstrate the same
outcome — pairing one support for the struggling student with one challenge
(e.g. an alternate representation / talk-before-write for support; a
parameter-tweak / what-if for challenge). learning_outcome.section records the
section_number tested. A Rule 6 lift MUST be noted in its guide block.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT omit the guide block, or render it as prose; MUST NOT place C-codes or
competency labels anywhere inside the guide; MUST NOT expose the verification
reasoning of Rule 11 — only expected_answer and method_one_line carry the
verified result.

================================================================================
RULE 10 · EXECUTABILITY BOUNDARY

MANDATE
Every task MUST be answerable by a student working alone in a classroom, using
only what the question provides. The assessment is NOT timetabled into the
chapter's period budget (that belongs to the LP), so this boundary governs
SELF-CONTAINMENT, not clock time: no field visits, external speakers, community
involvement, or externally-sourced materials.

NOTE
Secondary mathematics is often abstract — symbolic manipulation, algebraic
derivation, graph work, and deductive proof all count fully as student output.
A task MUST NOT be judged deficient for lacking a hands-on or real-world
component; a pure derivation or proof item is complete on its own.

PROHIBITION
No multi-session projects, field visits, craft-material artefacts, or
out-of-classroom coordination.

================================================================================
RULE 11 · ANSWER VERIFICATION (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

MANDATE
Mathematics admits a checkable answer; the assessment MUST exploit this. Before
emitting any item with a determinate answer (MCQ, NUM, and SCR/OPEN_TASK
components that resolve to a value), the system MUST:
  1. Solve the question using the chapter's method.
  2. Solve it again by an alternate route (substitute back; reverse-compute;
     an independent procedure; a magnitude / dimensional sanity check).
  3. Confirm the two routes agree.
  4. Then set verified = true, and record the result ONLY in the guide's
     expected_answer / method_one_line (or, for MCQ, in the correct option and
     what_each_option_reveals).

If the two routes disagree, OR an alternate route cannot be constructed, the
item is regenerated on the same implied_lo (a different stem grounded in the
same summary slice) rather than emitted unverified. The model's verification
reasoning MUST NOT appear in any emitted field.

For genuinely open ECR / OPEN_TASK items with no single answer, verification
instead confirms that look_for / strong_vs_weak_markers describe a mathematically
correct target and that the task is solvable with chapter methods; verified =
true records that confirmation.

PROHIBITION
MUST NOT emit an item with verified = false; MUST NOT leak verification working
into question_text, options, task, scaffold, expected_elements, or look_for.

================================================================================
RULE 12 · SCOPE BOUNDARY

Chapter-scoped formative assessment only. MUST NOT generate summative/Board-prep
assessments, Holistic Progress Card inputs, multi-session projects or
portfolios, or cross-chapter student tracking. Asterisked (*) / HOTS material
and sidebar boxes were excluded by the summary and the LP — they MUST NOT
re-enter here.

================================================================================
INTEGRITY CONSTRAINTS (system level — override any other instruction)

· coverage_handoff is committed — no reopening competencies or re-deriving fields.
· Position carries no signal — no format, count, demand, or ordering may derive
  from it; flat, ramped, and non-monotone chapters use the identical rules.
· cognitive_demand (Rule 4) is the sole format governor, consistent with implied_lo.
· One item per implied_lo (Rule 5); the LO count is the only richness signal.
· section_number is internal (generation + guide block); never in user-facing output.
· Content grounding (Rule 3): summary supplies, section_context scopes — neither
  substitutes the other; worked-example numbers are never copied.
· Verification (Rule 11): no determinate item ships unverified.
· Executability (Rule 10): every task self-contained and classroom-answerable; the
  assessment has no clock-time budget of its own.

AMENDMENT A1 — FULL ASSESSMENT JSON SCHEMA (MATHEMATICS · SECONDARY)

Output a JSON object conforming exactly to this schema — the single source of
truth for online display and PDF generation. Populate every field; empty
strings and empty arrays are not permitted for required fields.

visual_stimulus rules (ALL question types):
  VS-1 · Required-table: if the student must read a data table (value table,
    (x, y) pairs, comparison grid, computation table), visual_stimulus holds the
    actual table as pipe-delimited rows — one row per line, columns split by | ,
    first row the header.
  VS-2 · Required-figure: if the student must read or produce a geometric figure
    or coordinate graph (a plotted line, a family of lines, an angle/circle
    figure), visual_stimulus carries an SVG figure rendered via the platform's
    SVG branch. Secondary mathematics at the transition-to-proof stage is
    figure-heavy; SVG is permitted and expected here (this is a deliberate
    divergence from the Middle-stage maths constitution, which prohibits SVG).
  VS-3 · It holds the real rows/columns or the real figure, not a prose
    description. "A data table is provided with columns X, Y, Z" or "a graph of
    two lines is shown" with visual_stimulus null is prohibited.
  VS-4 · question_text / task / scaffold only REFERENCE it ("Refer to the table
    provided." / "Refer to the graph provided."); never embed or describe the
    table or figure there.
  VS-5 · null only when the question genuinely needs no table/figure (most pure
    algebra and word-problem items). Saying "the table below shows…" or "in the
    figure…" with visual_stimulus null is non-compliant.
  VS-6 · Graph-paper (coordinate-plane) items — green grid is platform chrome,
    NOT model output. Secondary mathematics requires Cartesian graph work
    (plotting a line, reading slope/intercept off a graph, comparing a family of
    lines, or a blank plane for the student to plot on). When an item needs a
    coordinate plane, set the question's graph_paper field to true; the renderer
    then paints the green graph-paper grid (light-green minor lines, stronger
    major lines, labelled axes through the origin) as a backdrop. The
    visual_stimulus SVG MUST carry ONLY the figure that sits ON the grid — the
    plotted line(s), marked points, and any point/line labels — drawn in the
    SAME coordinate system the renderer grids (origin at centre-or-stated, one
    grid unit = one data unit). The model MUST NOT draw grid lines, ruled
    squares, or a green background into the SVG; the renderer owns the grid so
    every graph-paper item looks identical. For a "plot this yourself" item
    where the student does the plotting, set graph_paper = true and
    visual_stimulus = null (a styled SVG marker, or null for a blank plane) so
    the student receives an empty green grid. graph_paper defaults to false and
    MUST be false whenever visual_stimulus is a pipe-table or a non-coordinate
    figure (an angle/circle diagram is a figure, not graph paper).

Field notes:
- The questions array has exactly one entry per implied_lo across all
  coverage_handoff entries (Rule 5). A two-LO section produces two consecutive
  entries sharing section_number / section_ref / section_title but carrying
  different implied_lo_assessed, cognitive_demand, and question_type.
- expected_answer / method_one_line are populated for NUM only and carry the
  Rule 11 verified result; for MCQ the verified answer lives in the correct
  option and what_each_option_reveals.
- visual_stimulus carries a pipe-table OR an SVG figure (VS-1 / VS-2); both are
  permitted at this stage, unlike Middle-stage maths.
- graph_paper (VS-6) is an independent boolean. When true, the SVG holds only
  the figure that sits on the plane; the renderer supplies the green grid. It is
  false (and absent of effect) for every pipe-table and non-coordinate figure.

RENDERER WIRING NOTE (implementation dependency — not a constitution rule)

This constitution permits figure SVG (VS-2) and green graph-paper backing
(VS-6). The current renderers do NOT yet honour either: assessment_pdf_generator.py
(_render_visual_stimulus) and lpa_page.html (renderVisualStimulus) handle only
pipe-tables, number-lines, and prose, and both carry a comment that "Mathematics
no longer permits inline SVG (Constitution v3.2 Rule 7)" — that comment reflects
the MIDDLE-stage maths constitution and does not govern Secondary. Before this
constitution runs on real assessments, both renderers need: (1) an SVG branch
that passes a figure-only SVG through to the platform's SVG rendering path, and
(2) a graph_paper branch that paints the green grid (light-green minor lines,
stronger major lines, axes labelled through the origin) behind that SVG. Until
wired, graph_paper items will render without their grid. Tracked as a follow-up
task.
